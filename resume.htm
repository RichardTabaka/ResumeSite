United States Army

I entered the Army as a Combat Engineer in March of 2018 and was given some amazing opportunities to do things I never could have imagined before I joined. My first year and a half was action packed and full of fun, spending weeks in the Desert then weeks in the woods getting rained on with some of the most amazing people I've had the pleasure of meeting.

After that, I was moved to more of a desk job and given experience on the operations side of things. I was assigned as my company's Digital Training Management System operator where I Tracked and maintained records for a company of over 120 Soldiers. This is where I gained some of my most applicable experience to life outside the Army. I maintained my company's compliance of Army standards for multiple different mandatory trainings by verifying which soldiers had recieved training within the fiscal, calendar and rolling year depending on the task.

I was also given the opportunity to identify and help fix key weaknesses within my company by compiling and manipulating data by section within my company. For example, I would calculate a 30/60/90 day out qualification roster, identifying 20+ Soldiers with qualifications that would expire soon and ensure that they attended the necessary training to prevent them falling out of standard.

Finally, the most rewarding task I had was ensuring the timely promotion of my fellow Soldiers. Every month I gathered every certification and qualification the Soldiers eligible to be promoted would need, getting signatures and confirming the accuracy of these documents. I would then read through about 10 Soldiers' summaries in their board packets, ensuring they were well represented by them and that their biographies were error-free. This allowed typically about 5 soldiers per month to earn their Promotable status.
